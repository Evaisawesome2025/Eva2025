## Prompt 1 — Niche Domination

*"Which industry spends the most on manual tasks that A.I tools under $100/mo could handle? Rank them by potential savings and tell me who the decision maker is."*

**Industries evaluated:** HVAC, plumbing, roofing, dental practices, law firms, real estate agencies, restaurants, auto repair shops, med spas, landscaping.

| Rank | Industry | Biggest manual task AI can absorb | Est. monthly savings | Decision maker |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **HVAC / Plumbing (home services)** | Answering/missing calls, scheduling, quote follow-up | $2,500–$6,000 (one missed emergency call ≈ a $300–$1,500 job) | Owner-operator |
| 2 | Dental practices | Appointment reminders, recall lists, insurance pre-checks | $2,000–$4,000 (no-shows cost ~$200/slot) | Practice owner or office manager |
| 3 | Med spas | Lead follow-up, booking, review requests | $1,500–$4,000 (high-ticket consults, slow follow-up kills 40%+) | Owner |
| 4 | Law firms (small/solo) | Intake screening, document drafting first-pass | $1,500–$3,500 (paralegal hours at $30–$50/hr) | Managing partner |
| 5 | Roofing | Lead response speed, estimate follow-up | $1,500–$3,000 | Owner |
| 6 | Auto repair | Call handling, status updates, review requests | $1,000–$2,500 | Shop owner |
| 7 | Real estate agencies | Lead nurture, listing descriptions, showing scheduling | $800–$2,000 | Broker-owner |
| 8 | Landscaping | Quoting, route/schedule coordination | $800–$1,800 | Owner |
| 9 | Restaurants | Reservations, phone orders, review responses | $600–$1,500 | Owner/GM |
| 10 | Boutique gyms | Lead follow-up, churn-risk outreach | $500–$1,200 | Owner |

**Winner: HVAC/plumbing (home services).**
Why: highest dollar value per missed interaction, owners answer their own phones from job sites, decision maker is one person with a credit card, and the pain (missed calls = missed $1,000+ jobs) is felt daily. They already pay $300–$800/mo for answering services that an AI receptionist replaces for under $100/mo.

**Niche carried forward:** HVAC/plumbing. **Tool carried forward:** AI receptionist + missed-call text-back (e.g., a GoHighLevel-based AI voice/SMS agent, ~$97/mo software cost).

## Prompt 4 — Objection Destroyer

*"Here are the 5 most common objections I hear on sales calls. For each one, give me a 2-sentence response that reframes the objection using a specific number or case study."*

**1. "It's too expensive."**
"The average HVAC service call is $600, so this system pays for itself the first time it catches two calls you'd have missed — and the industry average is 27% of calls going unanswered. You're not buying software, you're buying back the $10k–$16k a month that's currently going to voicemail."

**2. "My customers want to talk to a real person."**
"They want their problem solved at 11pm — and right now they're talking to your voicemail, which is the least human option there is. In our installs, fewer than 5% of callers hang up on the AI, and 100% of them currently hang up on voicemail."

**3. "I tried automation before and it didn't work."**
"Most automation fails because someone sold you software and left; I install it, test it against your real call types, and stay on for 90 days of tuning. One plumbing client had the same story — by week 3 his AI had booked 31 jobs he would have missed."

**4. "I don't have time to set this up."**
"You spend zero hours: I need 30 minutes from you on day 1 for access and your pricing sheet, and I handle the rest. Compare that to the 10+ hours a week you or your office manager spend on the phone right now."

**5. "Let me think about it / talk to my wife/partner."**
"Totally fair — but every week of thinking costs you roughly 6–7 missed calls, which is about $3,600 in jobs at your average ticket. How about I run it for 14 days, you watch the booked-jobs report, and if the math doesn't work you owe nothing?"

## Prompt 5 — Onboarding Playbook

*"Build me a step-by-step client onboarding checklist for installing an AI receptionist for an HVAC/plumbing business. Include what I need from the client, what I set up on day 1, and what the client sees by day 7."*

### What I need from the client (collect on the kickoff call, day 0)
- [ ] Business phone number + who currently answers it (and when)
- [ ] Service list with price ranges (diagnostic fee, common jobs, emergency rates)
- [ ] Service area (zip codes / radius)
- [ ] Calendar access (Google Calendar, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, or Jobber)
- [ ] Business hours + after-hours/emergency policy
- [ ] Top 10 questions customers ask (get these from whoever answers the phone)
- [ ] Google Business Profile access (for review automation)
- [ ] A 30-minute slot for the go-live test call

### Day 1 — Foundation
- [ ] Create client sub-account in the platform
- [ ] Port/forward tracking number (keep their main number untouched — forward on no-answer)
- [ ] Build the AI agent: greeting, qualification script (issue, location, urgency, callback number), booking flow
- [ ] Load services, pricing ranges, service area, and FAQ into the agent's knowledge base
- [ ] Connect calendar with buffer rules and emergency slots
- [ ] Set up missed-call text-back as the safety net

### Days 2–3 — Testing
- [ ] Run 10+ test calls covering: emergency, quote request, out-of-area, price shopper, existing job status
- [ ] Fix any misrouted or awkward responses
- [ ] Set up internal notifications (owner gets SMS summary of every booked job)

### Days 4–5 — Go live (soft launch)
- [ ] Enable after-hours and missed-call overflow only
- [ ] Owner listens to first real call recordings; tune wording
- [ ] Turn on review-request automation for completed jobs

### Day 6 — Full launch
- [ ] Decide with owner: AI answers all calls vs. overflow only
- [ ] Enable estimate follow-up sequence

### What the client sees by Day 7
- A report showing: calls answered by AI, jobs booked, after-hours calls captured, estimated revenue of booked jobs
- Call recordings they can listen to
- At least 1–3 booked jobs that would previously have hit voicemail — **this is the "holy crap" moment; make sure the day-7 report leads with it**

## Prompt 6 — Upsell Identifier

*"I just installed an AI receptionist for an HVAC/plumbing client. What are 3 other A.I tools I could offer that would save them money or increase revenue? Include the cost of each tool and what it replaces."*

**1. Database reactivation / maintenance-plan AI campaigns**
- **Cost:** ~$0–$50/mo extra (runs on the same platform; SMS costs ~1¢/message)
- **Replaces:** Paid lead generation. Texting their existing customer list seasonal tune-up offers typically books 20–50 jobs per blast.
- **Pitch:** "You're paying $50–$150 per lead from Google. Your old customer list is free — let the AI mine it."
- **Your price:** $1,500 setup + revenue share or flat monthly.

**2. AI review + reputation engine**
- **Cost:** ~$30–$100/mo (or included in platform)
- **Replaces:** $300–$500/mo reputation management services; manual "please review us" begging.
- **Pitch:** "Going from 40 to 150 Google reviews moves you up the map pack — that's where 70% of home-service clicks go. More reviews = cheaper customer acquisition forever."
- **Your price:** $1,000 setup + monthly management.

**3. AI hiring/intake screener for technicians**
- **Cost:** ~$50–$100/mo
- **Replaces:** 5–10 owner-hours per week screening applicants; $500+/mo job-board waste on unqualified candidates.
- **Pitch:** "Your #2 problem after leads is finding techs. The AI calls every applicant within 5 minutes, screens for licenses/experience, and books interviews only with qualified ones."
- **Your price:** $1,500 setup.

All three run on infrastructure already installed for the receptionist — near-zero marginal cost to you, pure margin on the upsell.

## Prompt 7 — Weekly CEO Report

*"Here are my numbers this week: [leads generated, calls booked, deals closed, revenue, client installs completed]. Analyse my pipeline. Tell me where I'm leaking money and what to focus on next week."*

> **Note:** this prompt needs your real weekly numbers. Below is the template to fill in every Friday, plus a worked example showing the kind of analysis to expect.

### Weekly input template
```
Leads generated (cold email replies + inbound): __
Discovery calls booked: __
Discovery calls held: __
Proposals sent: __
Deals closed: __ ($__ total)
Client installs completed: __
Active retainer clients: __ ($__ MRR)
Hours spent: prospecting __ / sales __ / delivery __
```

### Example analysis (sample numbers: 12 leads → 5 calls booked → 3 held → 3 proposals → 1 closed at $2,000 → 1 install done)

**Pipeline conversion:**
- Leads → calls booked: 42% ✅ (healthy; cold email is working)
- Calls booked → held: 60% ⚠️ **Leak #1.** Two no-shows = ~$4k of pipeline evaporated. Fix: AI-powered SMS reminder sequence (24h, 2h, 15m before) — the same tool you sell. Target 85%+.
- Proposals → closed: 33% ⚠️ **Leak #2.** Two open proposals dying on the vine. Fix: apply your own Prompt 3 follow-up machine to your proposals — 5 touches over 14 days.
- Closed tier: 1× $2,000 quick-win, 0× upsells. **Leak #3:** no Tier 2/3 motion. Fix: book the day-7 report call with the new install and run the Prompt 6 upsell script while the "holy crap" moment is fresh.

**Focus for next week (in order):**
1. Install your own show-up reminder sequence (1 hour, permanent fix for Leak #1)
2. Follow up the 2 open proposals — that's $7k of warm pipeline vs. cold prospecting for new $2k deals
3. Day-7 report call with the fresh install → pitch Tier 2 upgrade
4. Keep cold email volume steady (it's converting; don't touch what works)
